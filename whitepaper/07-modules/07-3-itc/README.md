# Integral Time Credits (ITC)

The Integral Time Credit (ITC) subsystem provides a non-transferable, contribution-based access mechanism that records labor participation while preventing accumulation, speculation, and coercive exchange dynamics.

ITC functions as a coordination signal rather than currency, enabling:

- equitable access to shared production
- cross-cooperative reciprocity
- labor transparency
- ethical safeguards against exploitation
- adaptive forecasting of social needs

Unlike monetary accounting systems, ITC is designed to:

- stabilize access rather than concentrate power
- reflect ecological and social constraints
- support collaborative production planning
- remain embedded within CDS governance and FRS feedback

## Conceptual Summary

ITC transforms contribution into access without enabling accumulation, ensuring that participation signals remain cooperative, transparent, and aligned with ecological and social realities.
