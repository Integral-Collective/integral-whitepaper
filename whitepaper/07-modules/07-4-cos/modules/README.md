

This directory contains the operational modules of the **Cooperative Organization System (COS)**.

Together, these modules transform governance decisions, design intelligence, and contribution signals into **coordinated, real-world execution** across the federation.

---

## Module Flow Overview

COS modules form a recursive execution pipeline:

1. **Production Planning & Work Breakdown**
   - converts design + governance into executable tasks
2. **Labor Organization & Skill Matching**
   - aligns available skills with operational requirements
3. **Resource Procurement & Materials Management**
   - ensures material availability and ecological compliance
4. **Cooperative Workflow Execution**
   - coordinates task progress and operational sequencing
5. **Capacity, Throughput & Constraint Balancing**
   - detects bottlenecks and dynamically adjusts flows
6. **Distribution & Access Flow Coordination**
   - routes goods according to access allocation logic
7. **Quality Assurance & Safety Verification**
   - enforces durability, safety, and performance standards
8. **Cooperative Coordination & Inter-Coop Integration**
   - synchronizes cross-cooperative dependencies
9. **Transparency, Ledger & Audit**
   - records operational events for accountability and learning
10. **Full COS Orchestration**
   - integrates all modules into a coherent execution protocol

---

## Architectural Function

The COS module set ensures:

- execution coherence
- physical feasibility
- constraint awareness
- cooperative synchronization
- operational transparency
- learning feedback into CDS, OAD, and ITC

---

## System Position

COS is the **execution layer** of Integral’s five-system architecture:

- CDS → legitimacy
- OAD → design intelligence
- ITC → contribution accounting
- COS → execution
- FRS → feedback and learning

---

## Conceptual Interpretation

If CDS is the **nervous system** and OAD the **design intelligence**,  
COS is the **muscular system** of Integral — converting intention into action.

---

## Important Note

Modules are **not strictly linear**.  
They operate recursively with feedback from:

- FRS anomaly detection
- CDS constraint updates
- OAD design revisions
- ITC labor signals

This recursive architecture prevents centralized control while maintaining coordination integrity.
