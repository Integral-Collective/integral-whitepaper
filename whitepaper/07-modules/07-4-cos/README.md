# COS — Cooperative Organization System

The **Cooperative Organization System (COS)** is the execution layer of Integral.  
It transforms validated designs, governance decisions, and contribution signals into **coordinated production, maintenance, and distribution activity** across cooperatives and nodes.

While CDS governs decision legitimacy and OAD governs design intelligence, COS governs **real-world work orchestration**.

---

## Core Functions

COS ensures that:

- production is coordinated without markets
- labor is matched to real operational need
- materials flow without price signaling
- cooperative workflows remain transparent and auditable
- capacity and constraints are dynamically balanced
- distribution follows access allocation rather than purchasing power
- safety and quality are verified across cooperative networks
- inter-cooperative coordination remains coherent at scale

---

## Architectural Role in Integral

COS acts as the **execution bridge** between subsystems:

- **CDS → COS**
  - provides decisions, priorities, and constraints
- **OAD → COS**
  - provides certified designs and lifecycle models
- **ITC → COS**
  - provides contribution signals and labor availability
- **FRS ← COS**
  - receives operational telemetry and performance feedback

Without COS, Integral would remain conceptual.  
With COS, governance and design become **materialized reality**.
