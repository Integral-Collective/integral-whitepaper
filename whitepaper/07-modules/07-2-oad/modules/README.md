
These files define the functional components of OAD:

1. Design Submission & Structured Specification
2. Collaborative Design Workspace
3. Material & Ecological Coefficient Engine
4. Lifecycle & Maintainability Modeling
5. Feasibility & Constraint Simulation
6. Skill & Labor-Step Decomposition
7. Systems Integration & Architectural Coordination
8. Optimization & Efficiency Engine
9. Validation, Certification & Release Manager
10. Knowledge Commons & Reuse Repository
11. Full OAD Orchestration

---

# Relationship to Other Integral Systems

OAD is tightly coupled to the broader Integral architecture:

- **CDS** — defines what must be designed and validated
- **COS** — executes production using OAD outputs
- **ITC** — derives labor reciprocity metrics from design decomposition
- **FRS** — feeds real-world performance data back into design evolution

OAD therefore acts as the **technical knowledge substrate** of the Integral system.

# Design Philosophy

OAD operates under the principle that:

> design is not a product — it is a continuously evolving commons.

As such, OAD emphasizes:

- transparency over proprietary control
- adaptation over static blueprints
- interoperability over siloed development
- ecological viability over cost minimization
- long-term maintainability over short-term optimization
